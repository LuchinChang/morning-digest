🔬 Deep Dive
A Control Barrier Function-Constrained Model Predictive Control Framework for Safe Reinforcement Learning
25 min
arXiv 2604.06463 · Posted April 7, 2026
A fresh entry from just three days ago that sits precisely at the intersection of your two areas. The authors propose PECTS (Probabilistic Ensembles with CBF-constrained Trajectory Sampling), which jointly learns stochastic system dynamics with probabilistic neural networks and Lipschitz-bounded control barrier functions, then enforces safety by folding the learned CBF constraints into the MPC optimization while accounting for model stochasticity. This is exactly the kind of work that blends formal certificates (CBFs) with data-driven learning (RL/MPC) — take notes on how they handle the uncertainty propagation; it's the weak spot in most safe-RL papers.

Talking with Verifiers: Automatic Specification Generation for Neural Network Verification
5 min
arXiv 2603.02235 · February 2026
A framework that lets users write specifications in natural language and automatically translates them into formal verification queries compatible with state-of-the-art NN verifiers. High fidelity to user intent, low computational overhead. Relevant both as a tool and as a research idea: your RL policies will eventually need human-legible spec pipelines too.
Formal Methods Read Trending
Neural Theorem Proving: Generating and Structuring Proofs for Formal Verification
4 min
arXiv 2504.17017 · 2025
A three-component pipeline: (1) LLM generates natural-language statements of code to be verified, (2) LLM generates formal proofs for the statement, (3) a heuristic module assembles the final proof. A nice contrast to the RLVR thread you've been following — here the "verifiable reward" is literally a theorem prover.
