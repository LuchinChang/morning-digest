Deep Dive
HypRL: Reinforcement Learning of Control Policies for Hyperproperties
arXiv / NeurIPS 2025 · MSU & collaborators · ~25 min read
Most RL formalisms only handle universal trace properties (∀*), missing agent interdependencies like fairness, opacity, and noninterference. HypRL extends specification-guided RL to hyperproperties — properties over multiple execution traces expressed in HyperLTL. It applies Skolemization to handle quantifier alternations and defines quantitative robustness functions to shape rewards over product MDPs, then uses standard RL to maximize expected satisfaction. Case studies show clear gains over purely trace-based approaches, making this directly relevant if you work on multi-agent verification or privacy-preserving policy synthesis.

Aligning Agents via Planning: A Benchmark for Trajectory-Level Reward Modeling
arXiv · 2604.08178 · April 2026 · ~4 min read
Rather than scoring individual actions, this paper benchmarks reward models that evaluate entire trajectories — think of it as verifiable reward at the trajectory level, which connects naturally to trace-based specification satisfaction. A timely companion to your HypRL deep dive today, asking: how do we systematically measure whether our reward model captures the right thing over a full execution?
