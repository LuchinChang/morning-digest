Karpathy Scored Every U.S. Job for AI Exposure — The Pattern Is Striking

READ
Fortune · Mar 15, 2026 ~5 min TRENDING

Karpathy "vibe coded" an analysis of 342 BLS occupations for AI exposure on a 0–10 scale. The key finding: higher-paying, knowledge-intensive jobs score highest. 42% of jobs score 7+, covering 60M workers. The interactive visualizer at karpathy.ai/jobs is worth exploring. (Notably, the GitHub repo was quietly deleted within hours.)

Why today: This went viral last weekend. As a researcher, it's worth thinking about what AI can and can't automate in research itself — and how formal verification could play a role in trust.

What Comes Next with Open Models

READ
Nathan Lambert · Interconnects · Mar 2026 ~5 min TRENDING

Lambert (post-training lead at AI2) argues that closed models will make leaps that open models can't follow in 2026. He frames modern AI systems as a mix of weights, tools, and harnesses — and says open models need a new identity beyond chasing the frontier. Important strategic context for anyone building on top of open-weight models.

Why today: If your research uses open models for RL experiments, this shapes what's realistic to expect. The "weights + tools + harness" framing is useful for thinking about your own experimental infrastructure.

The LLM Architecture Gallery

READ
Sebastian Raschka · Mar 14, 2026 ~5 min browse

Raschka compiled a visual gallery of every major LLM architecture from GPT-2 to today, with clickable high-res diagrams, fact sheets, config.json links, and from-scratch implementations. Covers GQA, MLA, SWA, QK-Norm, NoPE, Gated DeltaNet, and more. A reference you'll return to repeatedly.

Why today: Bookmark this as a permanent reference. Understanding architecture choices matters when you're designing RL environments that interact with these models.
