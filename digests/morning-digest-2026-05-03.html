Process Reward Models Meet Planning: Generating Precise & Scalable Datasets for Step-Level Rewards
arXiv (Apr 22, 2026)·~25 min read
Process Reward Models (PRMs) are the step-level cousins of outcome-only RLVR — they score each reasoning step rather than only the final answer. The chronic problem has been data: how do you label step-level correctness at scale without a verifier in the loop? This paper closes the loop by treating planning search trees as the labelling oracle: every node’s value is bootstrapped from rollouts under a fixed planner, and the resulting (state, advantage) dataset trains a PRM that generalizes back to free-form reasoning. Read this with one eye on its analogy to your own agenda — “a planner is a temporary verifier.”

⚡ Quick Reads · 15 min total
Agentic Verification of Software Systems (AutoRocq)
arXiv · revised Apr 11, 2026·~4 min read (skim)
First LLM agent for full-on program verification: AutoRocq doesn’t fine-tune on proof corpora — it converses with Rocq (the new name for Coq) inside an iterative refinement loop, asking the kernel for context and feedback after every failed step. Evaluated on SV-COMP and Linux kernel modules. Skim the architecture diagram and the failure-mode taxonomy in §5; that’s where the formal-methods bones are.
