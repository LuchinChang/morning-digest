Formal Methods for Safety-Critical ML: A Systematic Literature Review

DEEP DIVE
Frontiers in Artificial Intelligence (2026) ~25 min Promoted from bookmarks

A comprehensive SLR covering 2020–2025 on how formal methods have been applied to safety-critical machine learning systems. This is the survey paper your lit review needs: it taxonomizes approaches (model checking, theorem proving, abstract interpretation, runtime verification) and maps them to ML lifecycle stages (training, testing, deployment). The RL-specific sections cover shielding, constrained MDPs, and reward specification verification — all directly relevant to your research.

Promoted from bookmarks (queued since Day 4). This is the single most useful reference for positioning your thesis at the intersection of formal methods and RL. Read the taxonomy and RL sections carefully; skim the DNN verification parts. Save the bibliography — it's a goldmine.
⚡ Quick Reads (13 min total)

Safe Multi-Agent RL Using Formal Runtime Enforcement: A Case Study

READ
Mohapatra, Anand & Pinisetty / Springer LNCS (2026) ~5 min Formal methods × multi-agent RL

A 2026 case study applying runtime enforcement — a formal verification technique — to a multi-agent RL drone swarm. Enforcement strategies prevent collisions and boundary breaches while improving overall swarm performance. The key contribution: combining RE with MARL improves rather than degrades learning, countering the common objection that safety constraints slow down RL. Directly extends the shielding paradigm from yesterday's CACM survey into multi-agent settings.

Fresh paper, right at the intersection of your research. Builds on the Shields survey from Day 7. Read intro + enforcement mechanism design; skim the drone experiments.

AI and Bots Have Officially Taken Over the Internet

TRENDING
CNBC / Human Security Report ~4 min Published March 26, 2026

The 2026 State of AI Traffic report from Human Security shows automated traffic now exceeds human traffic online. AI traffic grew 187% in 2025; agent traffic (like OpenClaw) grew nearly 8,000%. Cloudflare's CEO predicts full crossover by 2027, but Human Security's data suggests it's already happening. The "dead internet" hypothesis is becoming empirical fact. Stu Solomon: "The internet was created with this basic notion that there's a human on the other side of the screen, and that notion is very rapidly being replaced."

Big-picture context for why formal verification of AI agents matters: when most internet actors are autonomous, trust and safety require formal guarantees, not just vibes.

Mistral Small 4: Open-Source Reasoning, Vision & Code in One Model

TRENDING
Mistral AI ~4 min Released March 16, 2026 · Apache 2.0

Mistral unifies reasoning (Magistral), multimodal (Pixtral), and agentic coding (Devstral) into a single 119B-parameter MoE model — but only 6B active parameters per token. A configurable reasoning_effort parameter lets you dial reasoning depth per request. 256k context window, 40% latency reduction vs. Small 3, Apache 2.0 license. On benchmarks it matches or beats GPT-OSS 120B with significantly shorter outputs. The biggest open-source model news of March.

Practical tool awareness: if you ever need a self-hosted reasoning model for RL experiment pipelines, this is the current best option. The MoE architecture + configurable reasoning is a neat engineering choice.
