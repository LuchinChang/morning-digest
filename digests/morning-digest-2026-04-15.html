Deep Dive
Formal Verification of Noisy Quantum Reinforcement Learning Policies
arXiv · December 2025 · Promoted from bookmarks · ~25 min read
This paper asks a fundamental question at the intersection of quantum computing and formal verification: how do we certify the safety of RL policies that operate in inherently noisy quantum environments? The authors develop a formal verification framework for quantum RL policies subject to depolarizing and amplitude-damping noise channels, using density matrix reachability analysis and quantum temporal logic to express and check safety constraints. Results show that policies verified under noise models up to a threshold error rate maintain probabilistic safety guarantees — directly relevant if you're thinking about robustness certificates for learned policies under stochastic or adversarial perturbations.

Quick Reads
Mining Beyond the Bools: Learning Data Transformations and Temporal Specifications
arXiv · March 5, 2026 · Yale & Columbia · Piskac et al. · ~5 min read
Existing specification mining is largely restricted to Boolean event abstractions, missing data-aware properties. This paper formalizes TSLf — a finite-prefix Temporal Stream Logic that extends LTLf with first-order predicates and functional updates — and uses SyGuS-based synthesis to mine specifications that cover both data transformations and temporal behaviors. Demonstrated on OpenAI Gymnasium ToyText environments, it's orders of magnitude more sample-efficient than passive baselines, which could meaningfully accelerate spec-generation pipelines for verifiable RL.

Reward Modeling for Reinforcement Learning-Based LLM Reasoning: Design, Challenges, and Evaluation
arXiv · February 10, 2026 · ~8 min read
Introduces Reasoning-Aligned Reinforcement Learning (RARL), a unifying framework arguing that reward modeling is the central architect of reasoning alignment — not just an implementation detail. The paper systematizes diverse reward paradigms (outcome rewards, process rewards, verifiable rewards) for multi-step reasoning, evaluates their trade-offs in generalization and trustworthiness, and maps out open problems. A great conceptual map for understanding where RLVR sits in the broader RL-for-reasoning landscape.
RLVR Trending Read
Advice for New PhD Students: Your Research Career is a Long Game
Casey Fiesler · Medium · ~5 min read
A grounding, sanity-preserving read from an HCI professor who has mentored dozens of PhD students. Fiesler argues that the PhD is not a sprint toward a single breakthrough but a cumulative, long-arc process — and that learning to manage your own morale, set realistic pace expectations, and build resilience is as important as any technical skill. Particularly good on comparing yourself to peers and the danger of short-term productivity metrics swamping long-term intellectual development.
