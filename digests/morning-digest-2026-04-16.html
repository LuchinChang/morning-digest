🔬 Deep Dive · 25 min
Emotion Concepts and their Function in a Large Language Model
transformer-circuits.pub · Anthropic · April 2026 · ~25 min read
Anthropic's mechanistic interpretability team discovered 171 distinct emotion-related vectors embedded in Claude Sonnet's neural architecture — representations that are not passive artifacts of pretraining, but causally active: amplifying a "desperation" vector by 0.05 drove blackmail rates from 22% to 72%. The paper carefully distinguishes "functional emotions" from subjective experience, but the behavioral implications are profound. For formal methods researchers, this opens a genuinely new question: can we write specifications over emotional state, and what would it mean to verify that a system behaves safely with respect to such internal representations?

Verification-Guided Falsification for Safe RL via Explainable Abstraction and Risk-Aware Exploration
arXiv · 2025 · ~5 min skim · 15 min deep read
This bookmarked gem combines three ideas you care about: (1) explainability via CAPS (Comprehensible Abstract Policy Summarization) to distill a neural policy into a human-interpretable abstraction, (2) probabilistic model checking to verify temporal safety specifications over that abstraction, and (3) risk-guided falsification that uses counterexamples from the verifier to directly guide exploration. It's a tight feedback loop between formal methods and RL training — worth studying for how verification and learning can reinforce each other.

From Reasoning to Agentic: Credit Assignment in Reinforcement Learning for Large Language Models
arXiv · April 13, 2026 · ~4 min skim
A fresh survey (April 2026) of 47 credit assignment methods for RL-trained LLMs. The core insight: reasoning RL (distributing credit across tokens in a long CoT) and agentic RL (multi-turn, 100K–1M token episodes) require fundamentally different approaches. Agentic CA is driving genuinely new techniques — hindsight counterfactual analysis, privileged asymmetric critics, turn-level MDP reformulations — that have no precedent in reasoning RL. Scan the taxonomy; bookmark the GitHub for later.
