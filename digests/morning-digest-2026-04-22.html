🔬 Deep Dive · 25 min
Neuro-Symbolic Generation and Validation of Memory-Aware Formal Function Specifications
arXiv · Mar 2026 · Jaffal, Kosmatov et al. · ~25 min
Sits squarely in your thesis sweet spot: LLM proposes candidate memory-aware function specs (ACSL-style) for C programs from a natural-language description, then a symbolic verifier (Frama-C / WP) hands back compiler & proof diagnostics that drive the next refinement. The interesting move is that the symbolic side isn't just a grader — its counter-obligations are fed back as structured prompts, so the LLM is nudged toward the provable spec rather than the plausible one. Read with two questions in mind: (1) what's the analogue of their refinement loop when the "specification" is a reward/shield constraint instead of an ACSL predicate; (2) how do they keep the pipeline from diverging when the verifier returns uninformative "unknown" verdicts? Both map directly onto spec-synthesis for safe RL.

Trending Agents Code Read
GAR: Generative Adversarial Reinforcement Learning for Formal Theorem Proving
arXiv · Ruida Wang et al. · OpenReview accepted · ~4 min
Neat twist on RLVR: instead of a fixed problem set, co-train a statement fuser (which generates harder-but-solvable Lean theorems) against a prover, with the Lean kernel as the reward oracle. That yields an implicit curriculum — difficulty tracks the prover's capability automatically. Reported gains: +4.2% pass@32 on MiniF2F-Test for Goedel-Prover-V2-8B and DeepSeek-Prover-V2-7B. Why it matters for you: the same "verifier-as-reward + generator-as-curriculum" recipe is a clean blueprint for spec synthesis where negative examples are expensive to curate.
