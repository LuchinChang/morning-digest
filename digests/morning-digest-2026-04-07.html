Deep Dive · 25 min

Policy Improvement Reinforcement Learning (PIRL)

DEEP DIVE
READ TRENDING
arXiv · Beihang University & Peking University · April 1, 2026 · ~25 min

Current RLVR methods (GRPO, DAPO, etc.) optimize policies using instantaneous batch statistics without verifying whether updates actually improved the model — an open-loop design that can silently drift or collapse. PIRL addresses this by replacing surrogate reward maximization with the explicit objective of maximizing cumulative policy improvement across iterations, introducing a Policy Improvement Policy Optimization (PIPO) algorithm. If you've been reading the RLVR debate from last week's digest, this paper directly responds to the "open loop" critique and is worth reading closely.

Evaluating LLM-Generated ACSL Annotations for Formal Verification

FORMAL METHODS
READ TRENDING
arXiv · ~5 min

Researchers tested GPT-5 and OLMo3 on generating ACSL (ANSI/ISO C Specification Language) annotations for formal verification of C code. The study directly benchmarks frontier models on the specification-generation problem that sits at the intersection of your two research areas — how well can LLMs produce the kind of formal artifacts that downstream verifiers actually need? Skim the evaluation tables; the gap between models is telling.
