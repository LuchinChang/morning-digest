Sample-Efficient Neurosymbolic Deep Reinforcement Learning
arXiv (Jan 2026)·~22 min read
Classical deep RL is famously sample-hungry; this paper argues the bottleneck isn’t the policy but the value function’s ignorance of structure. The authors inject symbolic priors — piecewise-linear region partitions, MDP-level invariants, and a pre-specified abstract transition graph — into the value approximator, so updates propagate through equivalence classes rather than one trajectory at a time. Reported result: 5–15× sample efficiency on long-horizon, sparse-reward Atari and procgen variants. Read this with the formal-methods eye: the “symbolic prior” is doing the same job a partial-order reduction does in model checking — collapsing equivalent states so the search budget pays off. The natural next question for your own agenda is whether a learned abstraction (from a verifier-in-the-loop) can do the same job at scale.

⚡ Quick Reads · 15 min total
Uniform-Correct Policy Optimization: Breaking RLVR’s Indifference to Diversity
arXiv · May 1, 2026·~4 min read (skim)
Hot off the press. RLVR (and GRPO in particular) reliably lifts Pass@1 but tanks Pass@K — probability mass concentrates on a single correct mode and alternative valid solutions die out. The authors prove that GRPO’s objective is invariant to which correct solution gets the mass, then patch in a uniform-over-correct-modes regularizer. Recovers Pass@K without giving up Pass@1. Skim §3 (the indifference theorem) and the GSM-style histograms in §5 — that’s the load-bearing evidence.
RLVR Trending Read
Latent-GRPO: Group Relative Policy Optimization for Latent Reasoning
arXiv · Apr 29, 2026·~4 min read
If your reasoning chain lives in continuous latent space (no tokens), what is the GRPO group? The naive port is unstable — advantages collapse because samples in latent space cluster too tightly. The paper proposes a Voronoi-partition-based grouping in latent space and an entropy-floor schedule that keeps the policy from sealing itself in a degenerate basin. Useful even if you stay in token-space land: it makes explicit which assumptions of GRPO are actually load-bearing.

Raschka’s up-to-date map of the RL-for-reasoning landscape: where RLVR sits, what GRPO actually does mechanically, why PRMs are creeping back into post-training, and the open questions on inference-time scaling. Use this as the orientation read before you dive into the two arXiv papers above — it puts them on the same axis.
