Today's Deep Dive (20 min)

Decoupling Reasoning and Confidence: Resurrecting Calibration in RLVR

READ
arXiv · Mar 2026 ~20 min DEEP DIVE

Promoted from your bookmarks. This paper tackles a critical failure mode in RLVR: models trained with verifiable rewards become dangerously over-confident in wrong answers. The authors propose decoupling the reasoning process from confidence estimation, so the model can still explore aggressively during RL training while maintaining calibrated uncertainty. This is directly relevant to any work combining RL with formal correctness guarantees — you need the model to know what it doesn't know.

Why today: You've been building up RLVR context over the past three days. This paper connects RLVR to calibration and epistemic humility — a bridge between learned reward signals and formal safety properties. Focus on Sections 1-3 and the experimental setup.
Quick Reads (15 min total)

Karpathy: Humans Are the Bottleneck in AI Research

READ
WinBuzzer · Mar 23, 2026 ~4 min TRENDING

Karpathy's autoresearch agent ran 700 experiments over two nights and found 20 optimizations he'd missed by hand — an 11% training speed gain on a larger model. His provocative claim: human researchers are now the bottleneck in any domain with a clear, computable metric. But he's careful to note the limits — this only works where evaluation is automated. For your PhD, the question is: can formal verification provide the "computable metric" that makes RL research more automatable?

Why today: Fresh take on the autoresearch story. Connects directly to your work — formal specs could be the automated evaluation signal that makes RL research loops tighter.

Anthropic Launches Claude Code Auto Mode with Safety Gates

READ
TechCrunch · Mar 24, 2026 ~4 min TRENDING

Anthropic shipped auto mode for Claude Code — a classifier reviews each tool call before execution, scanning for mass file deletions, data exfiltration, and prompt injection attacks. Safe actions proceed automatically; risky ones are blocked. It's essentially runtime formal verification of agent actions. Compare this with Meta's rogue agent incident from last week: different approaches to the same problem of constraining autonomous AI behavior.

Why today: A real-world deployment of "verify before acting" — the exact principle your research area formalizes. Note how the safety classifier acts as a lightweight specification checker.

Robust Deep RL Using Formal Verification (TASE 2025)

READ
Springer LNCS · 2026 ~5 min (abstract + method) YOUR FIELD

A new paper integrating formal verification directly into the DRL training loop. Counterexamples generated by verification tools serve as corrective feedback to guide policy adjustments, enabling the agent to avoid unsafe actions and learn faster. Experiments show up to 70% reduction in training episodes while significantly improving policy safety. This is verification-in-the-loop RL — exactly your intersection.

Why today: This is a paper in your exact research area. Skim the abstract, method overview, and results table. Consider how this compares to AlphaProof's approach from Day 3.

MCP Hits 97 Million Downloads: The Agentic Infrastructure Moment

READ
Digital Applied · Mar 25, 2026 ~3 min TRENDING

Anthropic's Model Context Protocol went from a handful of reference implementations to 97 million monthly SDK downloads and 5,800+ community servers. Every major AI company now supports it. This is the "USB port for AI agents" moment — and it raises interesting questions about formal specifications for agent-tool interfaces.

Why today: Infrastructure context. Understanding how agents connect to tools is useful background for thinking about where formal guarantees need to live in agentic systems.
