⚡ Quick Reads · ~15 min total
Shielded Reinforcement Learning Under Dynamic Temporal Logic Constraints
arXiv · Mar 2026 · Yüksel, Buyukkocak & Aksaray (Northeastern ECE) · ~5 min
A clean entry point into STL-conditioned shielding. The authors compose sequential control barrier functions with model-free RL to keep agents inside dynamic STL specifications throughout training — not just at convergence. Real systems need spatio-temporal constraints like "revisit the charging station every 30 minutes," and the standard "keep the reward below a threshold" framing breaks down fast. Worth skimming for how they handle the dynamism (constraints that shift over time) — that's where most shielding work punts.

Multi-Turn Reinforcement Learning for Tool-Calling Agents with Iterative Reward Calibration
arXiv · Apr 2026 · ~5 min
A practical find: naively dense per-turn rewards on multi-turn tool-calling agents hurt performance by up to 14 points. The authors introduce Iterative Reward Calibration — using discriminative analysis of rollout data to shape per-turn rewards without breaking advantage direction — and lift Qwen3.5-4B from 63.8% → 66.7% on Tau-Bench Airline. The 4B model ends up outperforming GPT-4.1 and GPT-4o at 50× the efficiency. Nice complement to yesterday's credit-assignment survey — this is a working instance of the problems that survey frames.
