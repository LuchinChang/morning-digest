Today's Deep Dive (25 min)

An Opinionated Guide to ML Research

READ
John Schulman ~25 min read DEEP DIVE

Written for the OpenAI Fellows program, this is the single most actionable guide for early-stage ML researchers. Schulman breaks research into three pillars: choosing problems, making progress, and personal growth. His key insight: goal-driven research (pick a measurable target, work backward) beats idea-driven research for most people starting out.

Why today: This is your Day 1 foundation. Schulman's framework will shape how you approach every other article in future digests.

"The Karpathy Loop": 700 Experiments, 2 Days, and the Future of AI Research

READ
Fortune · Mar 17, 2026 ~5 min TRENDING

Published yesterday. Karpathy released "autoresearch" — an AI agent that autonomously ran 700 experiments in 2 days on a single GPU, discovering 20 optimizations that yielded an 11% training speedup. This raises deep questions about the future of research methodology itself.

Why today: This is literally from yesterday. As someone starting a PhD in RL + formal methods, the idea of automated experiment loops is directly relevant to your workflow.

Ilya Sutskever: From the Age of Scaling to the Age of Research

LISTEN
Dwarkesh Podcast ~5 min excerpt TRENDING

Sutskever argues that 2020–2025 was the "age of scaling" and we've now returned to the "age of research." The next breakthroughs need better objectives, better generalization, and stronger safety — not just bigger GPUs. Meanwhile, LeCun says LLMs are a "dead end" and pushes for world models that learn through interaction.

Why today: This is the biggest strategic question in AI right now: scale vs. new paradigms. Your RL + formal methods background positions you at the frontier of this debate.

Why today: Maps the landscape you're entering — RLVR is especially relevant to formal methods (verification + RL = your sweet spot).
Bookmarks (5 min to skim, finish over coming days)

You and Your Research — Richard Hamming (1986)

READ
Full transcript · ~45 min read MULTI-DAY

The foundational talk on what separates researchers who do great work from those who don't. Hamming spent 40+ years at Bell Labs observing what made people like Shannon, Feynman, and Fermi exceptional. Core themes: courage, open doors, working on important problems, and "planting little acorns."

Spread this across 3 days (15 min/day). Tomorrow's digest will have a reading checkpoint.

A Survival Guide to a PhD — Andrej Karpathy

READ
~20 min read

Karpathy's retrospective on his Stanford PhD. Key advice: think of your PhD as more than papers, work from first principles, do things others don't do but should, and find your unique angle rather than following the crowd.

How to Get Rich (Without Getting Lucky) — Naval Ravikant

READ
Tweetstorm + essays · ~30 min total

Not about money per se — it's about building specific knowledge, leverage, and judgment. The mental models here (accountability, leverage, compound interest on effort) apply directly to a research career. A PhD is the ultimate "specific knowledge" play.

autoresearch — Karpathy's Open Source Agent Loop

CODE
GitHub · Mar 2026

The actual code behind the "Karpathy Loop." An AI agent modifies train.py, trains for 5 min, evaluates, keeps or discards. ~12 experiments/hour. Worth reading the README and understanding the architecture — you could adapt this for your own RL experiments.
