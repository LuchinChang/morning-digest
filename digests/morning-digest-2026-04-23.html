🔬 Deep Dive · 25 min
SymCode: A Neurosymbolic Approach to Mathematical Reasoning via Verifiable Code Generation
arXiv · Oct 2025 · Neurosymbolic + SymPy · ~25 min
SymCode has an LLM emit verifiable SymPy code instead of raw chain-of-thought: the Python runtime plus SymPy's symbolic engine becomes the judge, which gives you an externally-checkable execution trace rather than plausible prose. Reported gains are big — +13.6 pp on MATH-500 and OlympiadBench vs. CoT baselines — and the design is a near-perfect rehearsal for what you want in the shielded-agent setting: a symbolic artifact sitting between the LLM and the reward signal so failures surface as verifier errors, not silent reasoning slips. Read it with two questions: (1) how brittle is the extraction step that turns SymPy output back into a checked answer, and (2) can you port their "reject-trace" loss shape to RLVR where the verifier is a Frama-C obligation rather than a SymPy equation?

⚡ Quick Reads · 15 min total
Preguss: A Tale of 1001 LoC — RTE-Guided Spec Synthesis for Large-Scale Programs
arXiv · PACMPL (OOPSLA) Apr 2026 · Static analysis × LLM · ~4 min
Right next door to yesterday's Neuro-Symbolic Specs paper, but the "trick" is orthogonal: Preguss uses runtime-error analysis (from static analysis) to pick where the LLM should synthesize specs, then refines interprocedural obligations unit-by-unit. The headline is operational — 80.6%–88.9% reduction in human verification effort on real programs over 1 kLoC. Skim the evaluation: their "divide-and-conquer by RTE unit" is a cheap heuristic that might let you bound the LLM's scope in the shielded-spec pipeline you've been sketching.

Ax-Prover: A Deep Reasoning Agentic Framework for Theorem Proving in Mathematics and Quantum Physics
arXiv · Axiom · Lean + MCP · ~4 min
The new-ish pattern keeps showing up: general LLM + Lean tools wired through MCP, with the kernel as the reward oracle. What's interesting is the QuantumTheorems benchmark they ship alongside — proving Ax-Prover generalizes beyond contest math into a domain where background axioms aren't saturated. Competitive with specialized provers on the public benches, and clearly ahead on the new algebra & quantum sets. Useful reference shape for your "specialize a general reasoner with verifier tools" agenda.
