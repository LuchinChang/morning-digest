Automated Conjecture Resolution with Formal Verification (Rethlas + Archon)
arXiv · Apr 2026 · Ju, Gao et al. · Promoted from bookmarks · ~25 min
This is the paper you bookmarked on Saturday — today's the day. The authors build Rethlas (retrieval + hypothesis generation) and Archon (a Lean 4 resolution agent) to attack open mathematical conjectures end-to-end: generate a candidate, formalize it, and either prove it or produce a verified counterexample. The case study on Anderson's conjecture is the headline result, but the verification interface is the piece worth lingering on — how an LLM-in-the-loop search is kept honest by a kernel that never hallucinates. Trace the proof-obligation hand-off carefully; it's the same pattern you'd want for spec-synthesis + RL. Plan a second pass later in the week for §4 (the agent loop).

SpecRL: Reinforcement Learning with Negative Tests as a Completeness Signal for Formal Specification Synthesis
arXiv · Apr 2026 · ~5 min
Right in your wheelhouse: an RL pipeline that synthesizes Dafny specifications and uses the fraction of auto-generated negative tests rejected by the candidate spec as a completeness reward. It's a clever self-contained signal — the spec synthesizer doesn't need human labels, just a program and a counterexample generator. Worth a skim just to see how they shape the reward around rejection rates without collapsing to trivial specs. Good cross-reference for your own thoughts on spec-driven RL reward design.
Formal Methods RLVR Read
Data-Driven Synthesis of Probabilistic Controlled Invariant Sets for Linear MDPs
arXiv · Apr 3, 2026 · Hashimoto, Kimura, Gao et al. · ~5 min
Complementary to last Saturday's dynamic-STL shielding paper. Given an unknown linear MDP, the authors build a conservative, high-probability N-step controlled invariant set using regularized least squares + self-normalized confidence bounds, then extend it to continuous state spaces via a Lipschitz-based lattice abstraction. The usable output is a set-valued safe action map — essentially a learned shield with statistical certification. Useful mental model for bridging RL's data-driven world with the formal-methods world's invariants.
