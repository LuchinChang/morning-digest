Today's Deep Dive (25 min)

LeWorldModel: LeCun's JEPA That Finally Trains End-to-End from Raw Pixels

READ
MarkTechPost · Mar 23, 2026 ~25 min DEEP DIVE

This week, LeCun's collaborators across Mila, NYU, Samsung SAIL, and Brown introduced LeWorldModel (LeWM) — the first JEPA architecture that trains stably end-to-end from raw pixels without the usual "hand-holding" heuristics like stop-gradients, EMA targets, or frozen encoders. The trick: a SIGReg regularizer using the Cramér-Wold theorem to keep latent embeddings diverse and prevent representation collapse. The result is dramatic — 200× fewer tokens than foundation-model-based world models, enabling planning up to 48× faster (under one second for full trajectory optimization).

Why today: This is LeCun's contrarian bet in action. World models that plan in latent space rather than pixel space are deeply relevant to RL — if your agent can simulate outcomes cheaply, formal verification of learned policies becomes computationally tractable. Read the architecture overview and the ablation on collapse prevention.
Quick Reads (15 min total)

Amazon's 90-Day Code Safety Reset After AI Agent Causes 6.3M Lost Orders

READ
Creati.ai · Mar 13, 2026 ~4 min TRENDING

Amazon mandated that 80% of engineers use its AI coding tool Kiro weekly. Then on March 2, a Kiro-assisted deployment caused 1.6M errors and 120K lost orders. Three days later, a second outage lost 6.3 million orders. Amazon responded with a 90-day "code safety reset" across 335 Tier-1 systems: mandatory dual-human review, formal approval gates, and stricter automated checks. A vivid case study in what happens when you deploy AI agents without formal safety guarantees.

Why today: The gap between "AI writes code fast" and "the code is correct" is exactly the gap formal verification fills. This story is a real-world motivation slide for your research.

RL with Conditional Expectation Reward: Beyond Binary Verification

READ
arXiv · Mar 11, 2026 ~4 min (abstract + intro) TRENDING

RLVR works great when you have binary verification (correct/incorrect), but what about free-form domains? This paper introduces Conditional Expectation Reward (CER), which uses the LLM itself as an implicit verifier — computing the expected likelihood of generating the reference answer conditioned on the generated answer. Unlike binary RLVR, CER provides soft, graded reward signals that capture degrees of correctness. Works across both math and general-domain reasoning.

Why today: Extends your RLVR understanding from Days 3–4. The key insight — graded vs. binary rewards — connects to a fundamental question in your work: can formal specifications provide richer reward signals than pass/fail?

Microsoft's Zero Trust for AI: Securing Agents End-to-End

READ
Microsoft Security Blog · Mar 20, 2026 ~4 min TRENDING

Microsoft announced Zero Trust for AI (ZT4AI) at RSAC 2026, extending proven Zero Trust principles to the full AI lifecycle. Three core ideas: verify explicitly (continuously evaluate agent behavior), apply least privilege (restrict access to models, prompts, and data), and assume breach (design for resilience against prompt injection and data poisoning). They're releasing a new reference architecture and tooling in Agent 365. 80% of Fortune 500 now use active AI agents — and only 21% of executives have full visibility into agent permissions.

Why today: "Verify explicitly, assume breach" is basically runtime formal verification applied to enterprise AI. Notice how industry is independently converging on the principles your research formalizes.
