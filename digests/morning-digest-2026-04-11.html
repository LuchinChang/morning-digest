🔬 Deep Dive · 25 minutes
Robust Deep Reinforcement Learning Using Formal Verification
~25 min
SpringerLink · 2026 · Raviv, Vistuch, Gurevich, Dekel, Kugler
A 2026 paper that integrates formal verification directly into the RL training loop — counterexamples generated by verifiers are fed back as corrective signals, guiding policy updates away from unsafe actions. Experiments show up to 70% fewer training episodes needed to reach safety thresholds, plus substantially higher success rates on safety-critical benchmarks. This sits at the exact intersection of your research: a working blueprint for marrying formal guarantees with learned policies.

⚡ Quick Reads · ~15 minutes
Rethinking Exploration in RLVR: From Entropy Regularization to Refinement via Bidirectional Entropy Modulation
~5 min
arXiv · April 6, 2026 · Hengrui Gu et al.
RLVR policies converge too fast — a problem the authors call "restricted exploration." Instead of blanket entropy regularization (which is noisy and hyperparameter-fragile), they decompose entropy into "informative" (diverse solution paths to keep) and "spurious" (eroded reasoning to suppress). Their method, AsymGRPO, decouples treatment of positive and negative rollouts and beats strong baselines. A tight, must-read companion to the RLVR papers from the past week.

Research Advice Read
T³RL: Tool Verification for Test-Time Reinforcement Learning
~4 min
arXiv · March 2026 · Liao, Röhrich, Wang, Zhang, Samadzadeh, Tresp, Yeung-Levy
Test-time RL (TTRL) self-trains on unlabeled inputs using majority-vote pseudo-labels — but spurious consensus leads to mode collapse. T³RL fixes this by pulling in external tool evidence (e.g., code execution results) to reweight rollouts before voting, generating more reliable training signal. Large gains on AMC and AIME 2024, especially on harder problems. A natural complement to today's deep dive: verifiable structure improving RL.
