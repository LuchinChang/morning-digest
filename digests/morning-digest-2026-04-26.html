🔬 Deep Dive · 25 min
Broken by Default: A Formal Verification Study of Security Vulnerabilities in AI-Generated Code
arXiv · Apr 6, 2026 · 3,500 artifacts · 7 LLMs · formal verification · ~25 min
A large-scale empirical study that puts formal verification on top of LLM-generated code rather than relying on test passes. The authors run 500 security-critical prompts (CWE-aligned) through 7 production LLMs, generate 3,500 candidate programs, and use a verification pipeline (dataflow + SMT obligations) to certify or refute the security claim of each artifact. Headline finding: across the seven models, the rate of verifiably-secure outputs is meaningfully lower than what unit-test-only evaluations report, because tests miss the silent CWEs (use-after-free patterns, integer-overflow paths, missing TOCTOU guards). Read it with three questions tagged for your notebook: (i) the verification harness is published — can you swap their SMT layer for the Frama-C ACSL backend you're already running, and re-score on Qwen3.6-27B / DeepSeek V4? (ii) which CWE classes have the largest gap between "tests pass" and "verifier accepts" — that's the Pareto-frontier slice for an RL-with-verifiable-rewards loop. (iii) the paper's evaluation budget is dominated by SMT timeouts on the harder obligations; that's the same wall you hit with shielded-spec training, so the abstraction-refinement section is the most directly transferable piece. Pair this with yesterday's Semantic Equivalence Self-Play as your two-source baseline before you start drafting the proposal.

⚡ Quick Reads · 15 min total
Discover and Prove: An Open-source Agentic Framework for Hard Mode Automated Theorem Proving in Lean 4
arXiv · Apr 17, 2026 · Liu, Yin, Yuan et al. (PKU + Huawei) · ~4 min
Existing Lean 4 ATP benchmarks "cheat" by embedding the answer in the formal statement (Easy Mode). The authors release MiniF2F-Hard and FIMO-Hard, expert-reannotated versions where the prover must discover the answer first, then formalize it. Their DAP agent splits the work: an LLM does free-form natural-language reasoning + self-reflection to hypothesize the answer, then rewrites the statement to Easy Mode and hands it to existing ATPs. New SOTA on PutnamBench (first system to formally prove 36 theorems in Hard Mode) and CombiBench (10 vs. previous 7). Two reasons this matters for you: (i) the answer-discovery / proof-construction split is exactly the agent topology the Ax-Prover paper from Apr 23 was groping toward — DAP makes it concrete, with code; (ii) the rewriting step is a clean place to plug in an RL-with-verifier reward signal for "did the rewritten statement actually match the original?".
Formal Methods Agents Read
Online Reinforcement Learning for Safe Gain Scheduling in Nonlinear Quadrotor Control
arXiv · Apr 18, 2026 · Hasan Zahed, Tsai, Hariri, Rastgoftar · ~4 min
Instead of letting RL emit raw thrust/torque commands, the policy selects gain vectors online from a finite library of pre-certified stabilizing controllers. Safety is enforced two ways: (1) the action set is restricted to gains whose admissible region preserves forward invariance of a prescribed safe state set; (2) dwell-time constraints prevent the DQN from chattering between modes. The translational-isotropy trick collapses the action space; yaw is scheduled independently. The interesting design lesson for you: this is the cleanest 2026 instance of the "shield as policy quantization" pattern — the safety property comes for free because every reachable action is already certified, and learning only chooses among them. That structure ports directly onto a tool-using agent where the "library" is a set of solver-checked tool-call templates rather than continuous gains.

Reading today's open-closed performance gap
Interconnects (Nathan Lambert) · Apr 20, 2026 · ~4 min
Lambert pushes back on the Artificial Analysis Intelligence Index as a single-number proxy for "the frontier." His core argument: agentic-coding capability has begun to saturate the index, so the index increasingly tracks what's easy to benchmark rather than what enterprises are actually buying. He cites Gemini 3 — high index scores, modest deployment footprint — as the cleanest current example. Useful as the macro framing for the DeepSeek V4 release notes you read Friday, and as a reminder for your own work: when you propose the shielded-spec-RL eval suite, the harder methodological move is choosing benchmarks that are still informative at saturation — verifier-discharge rate per CWE, not just MiniF2F-style pass rates.
