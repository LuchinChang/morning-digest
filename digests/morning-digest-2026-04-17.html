🔬 Deep Dive · 25 min
SUPERNOVA: Eliciting General Reasoning in LLMs with Reinforcement Learning on Natural Instructions
arXiv · Apr 9, 2026 · Suvarna, Phan, Beikzadeh, Bansal, Gabriel et al. · ~25 min
RLVR has dramatically improved LLM reasoning on math and code — but it stalls out on open-ended tasks like causal inference, temporal reasoning, and common sense. SUPERNOVA proposes a data-curation framework that recasts expert-annotated instruction-tuning datasets as verifiable RLVR training signal, systematically unlocking general reasoning without needing synthetic verifiers. The key insight: instruction data already encodes rich reasoning patterns; the challenge is making them reward-compatible. A must-read if you're thinking about extending RLVR to formally-specified open-ended tasks.

⚡ Quick Reads · ~15 min total
From P(y|x) to P(y): Investigating Reinforcement Learning in Pre-train Space
arXiv · Apr 2026 · ~5 min
A sharp theoretical observation: current RLVR fine-tunes the conditional distribution P(y|x), but the reasoning ceiling is ultimately bounded by the base model's prior P(y). The paper introduces PreRL, which pushes reward-driven updates directly into pre-train space to break through that ceiling. Pairs nicely with the "Limit of RLVR" paper from your Day 1 digest — a consistent theme worth tracking.

Why Opinion on AI Is So Divided
MIT Technology Review · Apr 13, 2026 · ~4 min
73% of US AI experts see AI's job impact as positive; only 23% of the general public agrees. This piece unpacks the "jagged frontier" phenomenon — AI models that ace the Math Olympiad but can't read analog clocks — and why it causes such wildly different interpretations of the same technology. Good calibration reading if you're explaining your work to non-experts or thinking about how to position research claims.

AutoSpec: Automating the Refinement of Reinforcement Learning Specifications
arXiv · Nov 2025 · ~15 min
AutoSpec tackles a pain point you know well: hand-written RL reward specifications are brittle, and manually iterating on them is expensive. This paper proposes an automated pipeline to refine specifications using counterexample-guided feedback — essentially marrying ideas from formal synthesis with practical RL engineering. If you've been thinking about how to make reward shaping more principled, this is your read. Start with the intro and Section 3 today; the rest is strong follow-on reading.
