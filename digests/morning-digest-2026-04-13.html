🔬 Deep Dive · 25 minutes
Formal Methods for Safety-Critical Machine Learning: A Systematic Literature Review
~25 min
Frontiers in Artificial Intelligence · January 2026
A comprehensive systematic review covering 2020–mid-2025, categorizing formal methods for ML safety into eight families: reachability & over-approximation, SMT-based verification, MILP/ILP approaches, model checking, runtime verification, shielding, control barrier functions, and risk verification. This is the map of the territory you're working in — it will help you see where your formal methods + RL research sits relative to the broader field, identify gaps, and find collaborators. Skim the taxonomy tables first, then deep-read the sections on shielding and control barrier functions (most relevant to your RL work).

⚡ Quick Reads · ~14 minutes
An Imperfect Verifier is Good Enough: Learning with Noisy Rewards
~5 min
arXiv · April 10, 2026
How robust is RLVR when the verifier makes mistakes? This paper shows that noise rates up to 15% barely dent training performance across Qwen3, GLM4, and Llama 3.1. A model-based verifier at ~85% accuracy recovers most of the ground-truth signal. Practically, this means you don't need a perfect proof checker to get useful RLVR training — a significant insight for combining formal verification with reward-based learning.
Trending RLVR Read
Canopy: Property-Driven Learning for Congestion Control
~4 min
arXiv / EuroSys 2026 · December 2024 (published April 2026)
Learning-based congestion controllers are fast but unreliable. Canopy fixes this by introducing "quantitative certificates" — an abstract interpreter runs in the training loop, measuring the volume of an input region that provably satisfies safety properties, and feeding that signal back as reward. The result: controllers that are both adaptive and formally guaranteed. A concrete example of verification-in-the-loop training that maps directly to your research agenda.
